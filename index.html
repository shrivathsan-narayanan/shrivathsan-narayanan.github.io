Research Overview

My doctoral research focuses on numerical and experimental investigations of unsteady rotorcraft aerodynamics and gust interactions. The work integrates mid-fidelity free-vortex wake modelling, GPU-accelerated computation, and controlled experimental validation studies.

This research aims to understand transient rotor response under complex unsteady flow environments and develop validated modelling strategies for aerodynamic systems.

Experimental Systems

Development of a compact fan-array-based gust generation facility for controlled aerodynamic testing of rotor systems.

Rotor test rig for isolated rotor performance studies under controlled gust conditions using load measurement and statistical flow analysis.

Numerical Modelling

Development of a mid-fidelity free-vortex wake modelling framework for simulating unsteady aerodynamic interactions in multi-rotor systems.

The solver incorporates GPU-accelerated routines and hybrid modelling approaches for efficient large-scale parametric simulation studies.
