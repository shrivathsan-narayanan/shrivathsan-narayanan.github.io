About

I am a doctoral candidate in Aerospace Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology Madras. My thesis focuses on numerical and experimental investigations of gust effects on multi-rotor systems. My research areas include rotorcraft design and aerodynamics, unsteady flow behaviour, gust interactions, high-performance computing, and neural-network-based modelling for aerospace applications. I am particularly interested in extending these methodologies to broader interdisciplinary problems in aerospace, including aero-structural and fluid–thermal systems.

Research Overview

My doctoral research investigates the aerodynamic response of rotor systems subjected to controlled unsteady gust environments. The work involves development and validation of a mid-fidelity free-vortex wake method (FVM) model alongside design and implementation of a compact fan-array gust generator.

Numerically, the research evaluates rotor loads, sectional aerodynamic behaviour, and wake interactions. Experimentally, the generated gust field is characterized through statistical analysis and validation measurements.

  • Development and validation of a free-vortex modelling framework
  • Design and characterization of a compact fan-array gust generator
  • Combined numerical and experimental rotor performance analysis
  • CPU- and GPU-accelerated large-scale parametric simulations

Fan-array Wind Generator and Rotor Test Rig

A compact fan-array-based open-channel wind generation facility was developed to produce controlled gust environments. An isolated rotor test rig was designed to measure aerodynamic loads and validate rotor–gust interactions.
