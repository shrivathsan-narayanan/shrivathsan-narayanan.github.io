Research Overview

My doctoral research focuses on numerical and experimental investigations of unsteady rotorcraft aerodynamics and gust interactions. This work involves the development of mid-fidelity free-vortex wake methods for modelling transient rotor flows, alongside the design and implementation of controlled gust-generation facilities for experimental validation studies.

The research integrates computational modelling, large-scale parametric simulation workflows, and experimental measurements to understand rotor response under complex unsteady flow environments.

Experimental Systems

Development of a compact fan-array-based gust generation facility for controlled aerodynamic testing of rotor systems. The facility enables repeatable unsteady flow environments for rotor load and performance characterization studies.

Experimental rotor test rig used for isolated rotor performance evaluation under controlled gust conditions, including load measurement and statistical flow analysis.

Numerical Modelling

Development of a mid-fidelity free-vortex wake modelling framework for simulating unsteady aerodynamic interactions in multi-rotor systems. The solver includes GPU-accelerated computational routines and neural-network-assisted modelling approaches for efficient large-scale parametric simulations.
