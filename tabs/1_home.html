About

I am a doctoral candidate in Aerospace Engineering at Indian Institute of Technology Madras. My thesis focuses on the numerical and experimental investigations of gust effects on multi-rotor systems. I have also worked on high-performance computing for acceleration of numerical models, development of a neural network model for rotor inflow prediction, and on other practical aerodynamic problems for unmanned aerial systems (UASs).

My areas of interests include modeling and simulation of rotorcraft aerodynamics, experimental techniques for aerodynamics, unsteady fluid flows, gust interactions, high-performance computing, and machine learning for aerospace applications. I am particularly keen on extending my experience with numerical and experimental methodologies to a broader range of inter-disciplinary problems in rotorcraft, and aerospace in general, including design and optimization, aero-structural, fluid-thermal, and allied systems.

Research Overview

My doctoral research investigates the aerodynamic response of rotor systems subjected to controlled unsteady gust environments. The work primarily involves the development of a mid-fidelity free-vortex wake method (FVM) numerical model, alongside the implementation of a compact fan-array wind generator (FAWG) for experimental rotor-gust studies.

Numerically, the research studies the rotor loads and moments, sectional aerodynamic properties such as angle of attack and induced inflow, and rotor-wake interactions under different gust conditions. Experimentally, the generated gust field is characterized, followed by a statistical analysis of rotor loads and moments under real-time gust conditions.

Key components of the work include:

  • Development and validation of a FVM framework for multi-rotor simulations
  • Design, implementation, and characterization of a compact fan-array wind generator
  • Complementary numerical and experimental investigations of rotor performance characteristics under controlled gust conditions
  • CPU and GPU-accelerated computational workflows for large-scale parametric investigations
