Free-Vortex Wake Model

A mid-fidelity free-vortex wake method (FVM) framework has been developed to simulate the unsteady aerodynamic behaviour of multi-rotor systems under different flow conditions. The model represents rotor blades using lifting-line theory formulations and discretizes the wake into line vortex filaments to capture the induced flow and rotor wake evolution.

The developed FVM model has been validated for diverse flow conditions, and applied to rotor-gust problems, enabling detailed analysis of various aerodynamic characteristics of the rotor behaviour to gusts. Quad-Rotor Thrust Response

The developed numerical framework enables simulation of wake interactions in variable-RPM, multi-rotor UAV-scale systems, in addition to full-scale variable pitch systems. Representative time domain loading of individual rotors in a UAV-scale quad-rotor configuration is shown in the image below, alongside the wake structure corresponding to the peak of the oncoming gust. The results demonstrated the complicated rotor-wake interactions and the peaks in thrust response, indicating the influence of highly-unsteady flow patterns.
