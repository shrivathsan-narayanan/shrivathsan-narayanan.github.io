Free-Vortex Wake Model

A mid-fidelity free-vortex wake method (FVM) framework has been developed to simulate the unsteady aerodynamic behaviour of multi-rotor systems under different flow conditions. The model represents rotor blades using lifting-line theory formulations and discretizes the wake into line vortex filaments to capture the induced flow and rotor wake evolution.

The developed FVM model has been validated for various flow conditions, and applied to rotor-gust problems. Subsequently, various aerodynamic characteristics of the rotor behaviour to gusts have been studied in detail.

Sectional Aerodynamic Response for a Coaxial Rotor Configuration

Quad-Rotor Thrust Response

The developed numerical framework enables simulation of wake interactions in variable-RPM, multi-rotor UAV-scale systems as well. Representative time domain loading of individual rotors in a UAV-scale quad-rotor configuration demonstrate the complicated rotor-wake interactions and corresponding peaks in thrust response, indicating the influence of highly-unsteady flow patterns.
