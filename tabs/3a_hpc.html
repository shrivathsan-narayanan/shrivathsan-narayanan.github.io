GPU Parallelisation of Rotor Induced Inflow Computation

The free-vortex wake method (FVM) model developed for numerical rotorcraft aerodynamics has been optimised with parallel-computing techniques using CUDA FORTRAN. The profiling of the solver carried out using GProf in Python, revealed that a typical single core simulation of a full-scale single rotor hover solution spent nearly 70% of its time in the Biot-Savart induced inflow computations. The routine is an O(N2) operation, and can be directly parallelised due to the independent nature of the calculation of induced velocity by a given vortex filament on the other filaments present in the computational domain at a particular time instant.

The identified bottleneck in the Biot-Savart routine of the FVM solver has been optimised using CUDA FORTRAN by specifying the number of threads and blocks for computation. A full-scale single rotor hover solution has been demonstrated to achieve at least 2-3x performance gains when simulated on a AMD Ryzen 7 5800H CPU with a 4GB NVidia GeForce RTX 3050 GPU.

Further optimisation of other potential routines is currently underway.
