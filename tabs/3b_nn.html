Neural Networks for Rotor Inflow Prediction

The computationally expensive Biot-Savart induced inflow calculations in the free-vortex wake method (FVM) model primarily prevents large parametric studies in rotorcraft sizing and design stages from achieving mid-fidelity accuracy at rapid turnaround times. Low-fidelity blade element theory (BET) model yields quicker results but is however incapable of accurately resolving the rotor flow-field for complicated geometries and flight conditions. A hybrid neural network and BET model trained on FVM datasets, to predict rotor induced inflow can ideally achieve mid-fidelity accuracy at the cost of low-fidelity models, enabling rapid design and development strategies for complicated rotor systems such as variable pitch-speed, and hybrid multi-rotors.

The architecture of the neural network is also provided, which is based on the encoder-decoder concept, focusing on leveraging the least possible dimensions required to effectively represent the functional relation between the input and the output variable which is to be predicted.

Prediction of Rotor Inflow

The hybrid BET-NN model has been tested on hover, axial climb and descent flight conditions for various datasets in the train, validation (test) and a generic interpolated dataset outside of the train-test matrix. A span-wise constant initial bound circulation was provided for each case, and the model convergence was observed for the bound circulation in a fixed-point scheme, alongside the corresponding NN-predicted inflow. For each flight condition, the prediction of the both the span-wise bound circulation and the inflow demonstrated significant agreement with the FVM truth values across datasets.

Formulations for edgewise flight conditions are currently underway.
