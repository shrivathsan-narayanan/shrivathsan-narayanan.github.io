Fan-array Wind Generator and Rotor Test Rig

A compact, open-channel, fan array wind generator (FAWG) facility has been developed to produce controlled gust environments for aerodynamic testing in laboratory environments. A rotor test rig has been designed and fabricated as well, to measure aerodynamic loads and moments of isolated rotor systems subjected to gust conditions.

Flow Field Characterisation

Velocity data has been obtained across multiple, structured downstream planes to characterise the spatial structure of the experimentally-generated gust velocity field. A statistical and probabilistic analysis has been carried out to understand the evolution of the flow-field throughout the test domain.

Evolution of Turbulence Intensity

The generated gust velocity field has been evaluated using classical turbulence metrics such as mean velocity and turbulence intensity (TI). In addition, several other statistical and probabilistic metrics have also been computed to evaluate the spatial and temporal nature of the generated flow-field. Rotor Load Response

Experimental rotor-gust studies have been conducted to obtain the variation in the rotor loads and moments under unsteady gust conditions.
